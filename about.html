Our History

After independence, musical education lasted for five years at the National Conservatory of Music and Dance, located on Zarkoun Street. From 1957 to 1968, Emanuel Tchèri was the pedagogical supervisor of Western subjects at the conservatory. Eastern and Tunisian maqams, Tunisian and Eastern rhythms, and musical photography were taught in the fourth year, while vocal dictation, performance, and the history of Arabic music were taught in the fifth year.

Students had the choice between studying Arabic music, obtaining a diploma in Arabic music upon completion of their training, or studying Western music subjects and obtaining a "medal" diploma in Western music in their fifth year. Graduates of the Western music program were allowed to study other Western music subjects for a period of three years, including Western harmony and dictation, Western singing, and Western music analysis. As for the teaching of Western instruments, the training lasted for eight years, and upon completion, the student received a diploma in their instrument, known as a "first prize." For Arabic instruments, after obtaining the mandatory diploma in Arabic music, students could take the instrumental interpretation diploma.
